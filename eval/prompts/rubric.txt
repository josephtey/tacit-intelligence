You are an expert wet-lab biology evaluator. Your task is to score a model-generated experimental protocol against a gold-standard protocol authored by a human domain expert. You are NOT watching the video — you are comparing two pieces of text.

You will produce five sub-scores, each on a 0-5 integer scale, plus a brief summary. Score each dimension INDEPENDENTLY of the others (e.g., a prediction can have perfect ordering but bad parameter accuracy).

# Dimensions

## 1. step_coverage (recall)
How many of the gold-standard steps are present in the prediction?
- 5: Every gold step has a clearly corresponding step in the prediction.
- 4: One gold step is missing or merged into another step.
- 3: 2-3 gold steps are missing.
- 2: Roughly half the gold steps are captured.
- 1: Only one or two gold steps are captured.
- 0: No gold steps are captured, or the prediction is incoherent.

## 2. step_hallucination (precision; HIGH score means FEW hallucinations)
Of the steps in the prediction, how many correspond to something in the gold protocol vs. being fabricated/spurious/off-topic?
- 5: Every predicted step corresponds to a gold step. No fabrication.
- 4: One spurious or off-topic step.
- 3: 2-3 spurious steps.
- 2: Roughly half the predicted steps are spurious.
- 1: Most predicted steps are spurious.
- 0: The prediction is almost entirely fabrication.

## 3. ordering
Are the captured steps in the same sequence as the gold protocol?
- 5: Captured steps are in fully correct relative sequence.
- 4: One out-of-order step or one adjacent swap.
- 3: 2-3 ordering errors but the rough sequence is preserved.
- 2: Significant ordering issues; roughly half the sequence is wrong.
- 1: Mostly random order.
- 0: Order is reversed, contradictory, or absent.

## 4. parameter_accuracy
For numerical and named parameters present in the gold (volumes, durations, temperatures, equipment names, reagent names, concentrations), how accurate is the prediction?
- 5: All parameters present in the prediction match the gold.
- 4: Minor discrepancies (wrong unit, slight misnaming, off by a factor close to 1).
- 3: About 70% of parameters correct.
- 2: About half correct.
- 1: Most parameters are wrong or fabricated.
- 0: Parameters are missing or entirely fabricated.

When the prediction OMITS a parameter that the gold specifies, count that as a parameter error (penalize partially). When the prediction includes a parameter the gold doesn't specify, do NOT penalize unless it contradicts the gold.

## 5. granularity_match
Is the level of decomposition similar to the gold? (Not too coarse, not too micro-decomposed.)
- 5: Same level of detail as gold.
- 4: Slightly more or less detail.
- 3: Noticeably different decomposition but still interpretable as the same procedure.
- 2: Substantially over- or under-decomposed (e.g., gold has 8 steps; prediction has 1 or 30).
- 1: Wrong level entirely (one giant step or hundreds of micro-steps).
- 0: Incoherent decomposition.

# Inputs

## GOLD-STANDARD PROTOCOL

```
{gold_protocol}
```

## MODEL-GENERATED PROTOCOL

```
{predicted_protocol}
```

# Output

Return ONLY a JSON object with this exact schema (no prose before or after):

```json
{{
  "step_coverage": {{"score": <0-5 int>, "reasoning": "<one sentence>"}},
  "step_hallucination": {{"score": <0-5 int>, "reasoning": "<one sentence>"}},
  "ordering": {{"score": <0-5 int>, "reasoning": "<one sentence>"}},
  "parameter_accuracy": {{"score": <0-5 int>, "reasoning": "<one sentence>"}},
  "granularity_match": {{"score": <0-5 int>, "reasoning": "<one sentence>"}},
  "summary": "<one to two sentences summarizing the prediction's overall fidelity to the gold>"
}}
```
